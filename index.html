Global E-Commerce Impact

The explosive growth of online shopping has profound environmental consequences, from packaging waste to carbon emissions. These key metrics reveal the scale of the challenge.

Global E-Commerce Growth

Annual e-commerce sales have grown to $6.86 trillion in 2025, with continued increases projected through 2027. This rapid growth directly drives environmental impacts.

Primary Environmental Impacts

E-commerce operations used approximately 3.2 billion pounds of plastic packaging in 2023, with only a small percentage recycled.

Our Methodology

How we collected and analyzed the data behind these visualizations.

This dashboard presents data collected from authoritative sources including industry reports (Pitney Bowes parcel index, Statista), academic and government studies (OECD, EPA, IPCC), company sustainability disclosures (Amazon sustainability report, Oceana analysis), and research organizations.

Calculations for emissions estimates follow standardized methodologies from the IPCC and other environmental research bodies. Packaging estimates are derived from industry data and peer-reviewed studies on material flows and waste generation.

Projections are based on compound annual growth rate (CAGR) models applied to verified baseline data, with scenarios reflecting different possible growth trajectories.
